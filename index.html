Android Mascot History

You probably know that the Android operating system, powered by Google is one of the most used for mobile devices worldwide. But maybe you don't know that your friendly pet has a name and a very curious history? Well, follow this article to learn a lot about this little robot.

The first version

The first attempt at creating a mascot came in 2007 and came from a developer named Dan Morrill. He says that he opened Inkscape (free software for vectorizing images) and created his own version of a robot. The goal was just to personify the system just for his team, there was no request from the company to create a mascot.

This first bizarre version was even named after its creator: they would be the Dandroids.

A new pet appears

The idea of having a mascot matured and the mission was passed on to a professional in the area. The Russian illustrator Irina Blok , also a Google employee, was given the mission to represent the little robot in a more pleasant way.

Irina's main idea was to represent everything graphically with few strokes and in a flatter way. The drawing should also generate quick identification with the viewer. Then came Bugdroid, Android's new mascot.

Do you want to learn more?

Another curious thing about Android is that each version has always been named after a candy, in alphabetical order from version 1.5 to 9.0.

  • Cupcake
  • Donut
  • Eclair
  • Froyo
  • Gingerbread
  • Honeycomb
  • Ice Cream Sandwich
  • Jelly Bean
  • KitKat
  • Lolipop
  • Marshmallow
  • Nougat
  • Oreo
  • Pie

Unfortunately, the Android Q did not exist, as Google decided to put an end to this fun practice and started using numbers, which gave rise to Android 10.

visit the website Android History to see the sequence of "sweetened" versions and what each one brought to the Android system.
